Funding your elective

UK bursaries and prizes you can stack to offset the cost, plus Canada-side cost-savers. Tick "Applied" as you go — it syncs with your tracker. Each card has who it's for, the deadline, and a link to apply.

Honest framing: no funder pays for "Canada" specifically — it's a high-income destination, so several classic elective funds (which target low-resource settings) don't apply. The realistic play is stacking 2–4 small awards (£250–£1,000 each) plus free indemnity, for roughly £1,000–£2,000 off the budget. Most deadlines run annual spring cycles and several are unconfirmed — treat autumn 2026 as the window to re-check and apply.

Stacking strategy

  1. Watch + apply: RCP elective bursary the moment it reopens (~late autumn 2026). Join the RCP as a student member first. £500, strong fit.
  2. Ask UEA's own elective fund — nearly every UK school has internal bursaries (£200–£1,000). Email UEA's electives / scholarships office. High priority, easy ask.
  3. Build a small project or audit into the elective — that unlocks the project-based awards (BMDST/RSM £500–£1,000, IME £800 if there's an ethics angle, Simmonds £250).
  4. Play your specialties: a neuropsychiatry angle → RCPsych Neuropsychiatry (£200) + BGS (£500, old-age); a paediatric haematology-oncology angle → BSH (up to £1,000).
  5. Free indemnity: join MPS or MDU as a student (free) for elective cover — and ask whether it extends to Canada.
